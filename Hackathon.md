# Behind Alex.OS

Alex.OS began with a personal question:

> How could I honor BTS’s comeback year by building something meaningful from the fields I love most—technology, cybersecurity, and software engineering?

While studying cybersecurity and software engineering, I often encountered tools that identified problems but did not explain them in a way that helped me become a better developer.

I also remembered the difficulties I faced when I chose to pursue the field I truly loved. BTS gave me the courage to move away from the medical path and build a future in technology. With Alex.OS, I wanted to turn some of the challenges I had experienced into a solution for other learners.

I greatly admire Sam Altman and consider him one of my inspirations. I joined the hackathon to broaden my perspective on technology, learn more about building with OpenAI tools, and challenge myself to create a complete working product.

Throughout the hackathon, Alex.OS evolved through planning, experimentation, testing, and continuous refinement. AI tools accelerated the development process by supporting implementation, brainstorming, debugging, and documentation. All product direction, feature selection, testing, and final decisions remained my responsibility.

Rather than building another code-review tool, I wanted to create an educational experience—one that explains not only **what** is wrong, but also **why** it matters, how it could be exploited, and how developers can write more secure code in the future.

This hackathon MVP is the first public step toward a much larger vision for Alex.OS.
